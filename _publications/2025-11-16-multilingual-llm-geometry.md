    <div class="arxiv-section-title">Abstract</div>
    <div class="arxiv-abstract">
      This paper investigates how language-specific directions align with the geometric structure of token representations in multilingual large language models. We analyze the relationship between language directions and token geometry across multiple layers, revealing that multilingual LLMs develop structured geometric patterns where language-specific information is encoded in systematic directions within the representation space. Our findings provide insights into how multilingual models organize linguistic knowledge and suggest implications for cross-lingual transfer and multilingual model interpretability.
