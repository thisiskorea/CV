    <div class="arxiv-section-title">Abstract</div>
    <div class="arxiv-abstract">
      This work investigates how pretraining shapes inter-layer coordination in large language models by analyzing the evolution of Lie brackets between layer-wise transformations. We demonstrate that pretraining primarily builds cooperation between layers rather than refining individual components, revealing that the functional composition of layers develops structured algebraic relationships through training. Our analysis provides new insights into the internal dynamics of transformer pretraining and the emergence of coordinated representations across layers.
