    <div class="arxiv-section-title">Abstract</div>
    <div class="arxiv-abstract">
      This paper presents an analysis of linear separability and feature importance within the representation spaces of large language models (LLMs). We investigate how different layers of LLMs encode information by examining the linear separability of representations across various linguistic and semantic tasks. Through systematic feature importance analysis, we identify which dimensions of the representation space contribute most significantly to downstream task performance. Our findings reveal structured patterns in how LLMs organize knowledge, providing a deeper understanding of their internal representations and offering practical guidance for efficient model adaptation.
