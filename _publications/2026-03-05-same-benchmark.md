    <div class="arxiv-section-title">Abstract</div>
    <div class="arxiv-abstract">
      We investigate the phenomenon of task-selective convergence in LLM representations, where different language models converge to similar subspaces when evaluated on the same benchmark tasks. Our analysis reveals that despite differences in architecture and training data, LLMs develop structurally similar internal representations for specific tasks, suggesting the existence of universal computational patterns in language understanding. These findings have implications for transfer learning, model comparison, and understanding the fundamental nature of learned representations.
