    <div class="arxiv-section-title">Abstract</div>
    <div class="arxiv-abstract">This paper investigates the phenomenon of task-selective convergence in LLM representations, showing that different language models converge to similar representational subspaces when evaluated on the same benchmark tasks. Through analysis of hidden state geometries across multiple models and tasks, we demonstrate that benchmark-specific subspaces emerge consistently, suggesting that LLMs develop shared internal strategies for common evaluation tasks regardless of their training differences.</div>
